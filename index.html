WHAT IS THIS
Sandscape is a living terrarium simulator. You build an environment from sand, water, clay and nutrients, then introduce five kingdoms of life that compete, cooperate, and evolve. Every organism has a genome — genes that drift over generations, creating new strains adapted to your specific terrarium. No two runs are the same.
The board obeys physical gravity at all times. Rotating the box with ↺↻ tips the entire world — sand slides, water pools, organisms fall and find new surfaces. Use this to reshape the ecosystem dynamically.
THE FOOD WEB
The ecosystem runs on a single closed energy loop. Break any link and the whole system eventually collapses:
☀ SUN → 🌿 PLANTS → 🐜 ANTS → 👑 QUEEN → 🐜 ANTS → 🕷 SPIDERS → 💀 DEATH → 🍂 DETRITUS → 🍄 FUNGI → 🪲 MITES → 🐜 ANTS
The sun is the only external energy input. Everything else recycles. If the sun is off, plants starve, ants starve, the whole web collapses within a few hundred ticks.
THE FIVE KINGDOMS
🌿 PLANT
Grows toward the sun one cell at a time. Roots in sand, gold sand, or detritus. Older cells harden into woody stems (plant wall) which slowly drop leaf litter (detritus). Eaten by ants — without plant coverage, ants starve.
🐜 ANT
Falls through air, walks on all solid surfaces. Eats plants and mites. When energy reaches 220 with no queen nearby, an ant spontaneously crowns a new queen. Feeds queens it passes near. Tunnels through hardened clay, leaving open passages.
👑 QUEEN
Stationary hub. Spawns worker ants on a timer; faster with high repro gene. Lays eggs that hatch into ants. Dies without ants feeding it. The colony lives or dies with its queen — protect her or ensure ants can reach her.
🕷 SPIDER
Falls through air but clings to walls, plant walls, and web. Hunts ants and mites using venom. Lays web as it moves, creating climbing highways. Reproduces slowly. Too many spiders will crash the ant population — check their numbers.
🍄 FUNGI
Spreads through dark damp zones. Decomposes detritus back into sand, closing the nutrient cycle. Killed by light. Releases spores that drift upward and establish new colonies. Without fungi, detritus accumulates and nutrients lock up.
🪲 MITE
Fast-moving fungus-eaters. Kept in check by ants and spiders. Without mites, fungal blooms can take over the entire dark zone and choke out the substrate. Without fungi, mites starve and disappear.
HOW TO START A BALANCED ECOSYSTEM
Step 1: Use the STAMP tool to place a BOX or BOWL container. Add sand, water, and some gold sand inside for nutrients.
Step 2: Place 8–12 plants near the bottom. They need sand or detritus to root in and light to grow. Move the sun with the ☀ SUN tool to point at them.
Step 3: Drop 10–15 ants. They'll forage the plants and eventually crown a queen when well-fed. Watch population bars — you want plants to stay ahead of ants.
Step 4: Add 5–10 fungi in dark areas away from direct sun (bottom corners, under overhang). They'll grow slowly if detritus is present.
Step 5: Add 8–10 mites near fungal zones to prevent blooms. Add 2–4 spiders last — spiders are powerful and will crash an under-established ant colony.
Step 6: Drop 3–5 Life Seeds ⚛ near gold sand. They'll self-replicate and drift into your organisms, gradually evolving their genomes.
KEEPING POPULATIONS ALIVE
Plants dying? Check sun position — use ☀ SUN tool to aim the sun at your plant zone. Add more gold sand near roots. Make sure they're not buried under sand with no light access.
Ants dying? They need plants to eat. If plants are gone, add more manually. Queens starve if no ants feed them — if the queen dies, ants will eventually crown a new one when well-fed enough.
No queens spawning? Ants need to reach energy 220 to crown a queen. Make sure they're eating — add more plants. The ant population needs to be stable before queens appear.
Spiders eating everything? Add more ants, or manually erase some spiders. Spiders reproduce slowly so a brief population spike is self-correcting if ant numbers recover.
Fungi taking over? Add mites to dark zones. Move the sun to illuminate dark areas. Fire also kills fungi. Increasing light kills shade-obligate strains.
Everything dead? Add detritus to the substrate, re-seed with 🌱 SEED ECOSYSTEM, or reset and try again with a better container design.

EVOLUTION & GENOMES
Every organism has a 6-gene genome. Genes drift slightly each time an organism reproduces, creating new strains. The KINGDOMS ledger on the right tracks active strains — click any entry to inspect its full genome and traits.
Life Seeds accelerate evolution by touching organisms and shifting one gene in the direction of the seed's current recipe. Farm Life Seeds near gold sand or detritus to produce more. Place different seeds in separate fridge containers to maintain different evolutionary pressures you can deploy strategically.
Box rotation changes where "down" is. Organisms fall, settle, and climb in the new gravity direction. This is a powerful tool for reshaping the ecosystem — a flood of sand can bury a colony, rotating can expose a new water source, or scatter organisms across a fresh substrate.
